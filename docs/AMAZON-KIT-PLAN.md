# Kit plan vs VitalGauge requirements

**Question:** Can this Amazon base support a detailed Rest · Nutrition · Exercise plan that feeds VitalGauge inputs and targets?

**Answer:** **Partially.** This base is a **nutrition / supplement kit**. It can support **protein + vitamin/mineral coverage** in the app’s Nutrition targets. It **cannot alone** supply the DIY **measurement** inputs VitalGauge needs (pH, HR, BP, sleep, weight trends, exercise burn).

## 3. Suggested detailed plan (using this base + app)

### Daily (Nutrition — what the kit covers)

1. **Open VitalGauge** → enter demographics + any vitals you *can* measure → get targets (protein g, calories, sleep hrs, vitamin/mineral tables).  
2. **Protein:** Use whey to close the gap between food protein and the app’s **protein (g)** target.  
   - Example: if target is 95 g and food gives ~55 g, mix ~1 scoop (~20–25 g protein — check your label) 1–2×/day in the Strada.  
3. **Fluids:** Use the 24 oz Strada as one hydration block toward the app’s water target (often ~2+ L/day educational).  
4. **Multivitamin:**  
   - Male → ADAM 1 tablet with food (per label).  
   - Female → EVE as labeled (often 3 tablets with food — follow bottle).  
5. **Vitamin D3 10,000 IU:** **Do not treat as a casual daily default.**  
   - App educational D targets are typically **600–800 IU/day** for many adults.  
   - **10,000 IU is high potency.** Use only if a clinician/labs support it; otherwise choose a lower-dose D3 or skip.  
   - This kit **overshoots** safe DIY self-dosing for D without bloodwork.

### Rest / Exercise (app plan; kit does not measure)
- Follow VitalGauge **Rest** sleep band and **Exercise** kcal/walk/strength plan.  
- Log sleep hours and movement in the app (Plus fields help); kit products don’t replace a cuff, strips, or tracker.

### Weekly checklist
| Day | Protein shake | Multi (ADAM/EVE) | D3 | Enter app metrics |
|-----|---------------|------------------|----|-------------------|
| Most days | As needed to hit protein g | Yes with food | Only if clinician-approved | Weight / sleep / activity as available |
| 1–2×/week | — | — | — | BP seated + standing, RHR, pH if you add tools |

## 4. Gaps — add these to fully meet requirements

Minimum **measurement add-ons** (not in the 5 ASINs):

1. **Siemens Multistix 10 SG** ([B06WCZVMLC](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B06WCZVMLC)) — log all 10 pads in VitalGauge demographics  
2. **Saliva pH strips** (wide-range, e.g. 4.5–9)  
3. **Upper-arm BP monitor** (for seated + standing orthostatic checks)  
4. **Way to get resting HR** (watch, fingertip pulse ox, or 60-second pulse)  
5. **Bathroom scale** (if weight isn’t already known)  
6. Optional: **fitness tracker** or phone walk tracking for exercise kcal  
7. Optional: **lower-dose Vitamin D3 (e.g. 1,000–2,000 IU)** if not using labs for 10,000 IU  

Without Multistix + BP/HR tools, VitalGauge’s free vitals/urine inputs stay empty or estimated.

## 5. Safety notes (non-negotiable)

- Kit products are **not medical devices** and do not diagnose disease.  
- **EVE contains iron** — wrong choice for many men; ADAM/EVE are sex-specific.  
- **D3 10,000 IU** can be excessive for unsupervised daily use — clinician/labs first.  
- Multivitamins ≠ hitting food-based amino-acid targets; whey + diet still matter.  
- FDA disclaimer applies to all supplements on these listings.
